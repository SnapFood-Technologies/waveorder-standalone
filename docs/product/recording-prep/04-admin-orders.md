# Video 04 — Admin: orders

**Demo business:** RETAIL (see [README](./README.md)).

**Goal:** Show **order list**, open **one order**, and **status / fulfillment** actions your UI supports (e.g. confirm, ready, complete — match real labels).

**Target length:** ~3–6 min after edit.

---

## On-screen flow (record in order)

| Step | What to show | Notes |
|------|----------------|-------|
| 1 | **Orders** (or **All Orders**) | List view; filter optional |
| 2 | Click **one order** | Line items, customer, totals |
| 3 | Change **status** or equivalent | One clear transition |
| 4 | Optional | **Production queue** if enabled and relevant to retail demo |

---

## Script (read aloud or memorize)

**You** = merchant. **~** = pause. Use the **exact status names** your screen shows (e.g. pending, confirmed, completed).

### Order list (~45 s)

Open **Orders** — **All Orders** if you use a submenu. **~** Every **retail** order from your storefront lands here in **chronological** order. **~** You can scan **customer**, **total**, **time**, and **status** without opening each thread in WhatsApp. **~** This is your **operational inbox** for fulfillment.

### Order detail (~60 s)

Click **one order**. **~** You see **line items**, **quantities**, **prices**, **customer contact**, and **delivery or pickup** details if they applied. **~** That’s the same structured information your customer built in the storefront — **~** you’re not reconstructing it from chat.

### Status / next step (~45 s)

Update **status** to match your workflow — **received**, **preparing**, **ready**, **completed** — whatever labels **WaveOrder** shows for your store. **~** That keeps **you** and **your customer** aligned on where the order stands. **~** If you use **Production queue**, mention that **kitchen or prep** view only if it’s enabled and you’re showing it on screen.

### Close (~10 s)

That’s **managing orders** for **retail** — list, detail, and **status** in one place.
