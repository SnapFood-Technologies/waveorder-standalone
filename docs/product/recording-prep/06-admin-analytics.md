# Video 06 — Admin: analytics

**Demo business:** RETAIL (see [README](./README.md)).

**Goal:** Show **dashboard / analytics** with one clear **“so what”** (e.g. bestsellers, busy hours, repeat buyers) — not every widget.

**Target length:** ~3–5 min after edit.

---

## On-screen flow (record in order)

| Step | What to show | Notes |
|------|----------------|-------|
| 1 | **Dashboard** or **Analytics** entry | What merchants see first |
| 2 | **One** insight story | e.g. top products OR peak times OR repeat customers |
| 3 | Optional second screen | Only if it reinforces the same story |

Requires **PRO** (or equivalent) if analytics is plan-gated in your environment.

---

## Script (read aloud or memorize)

**You** = merchant. **~** = pause. **Only describe numbers and charts that are visible** on your demo account — don’t invent metrics.

### Entry (~30 s)

Open **Dashboard** or **Analytics** — on **Pro**, this is where **retail** performance comes together. **~** The goal isn’t to drown in charts — **~** it’s to answer **one** practical question per day: **what sold**, **when it gets busy**, or **who comes back**.

### One clear story (~90 s)

Pick **one** thread and stay on it. **~** Example A — **bestsellers**: point at **top products** or views so **you** know what to **stock** and **promote**. **~** Example B — **peak times**: show **when orders spike** so **you** staff or prep accordingly. **~** Example C — **repeat customers**: if the UI highlights **returning buyers**, tie it to **relationships** you’re building outside marketplaces. **~** Use **your** real widget names as they appear.

### Close (~15 s)

That’s **analytics** for **retail** on **WaveOrder** — **enough insight to act**, without a data-science degree.
